Insect Motion on Expanding Circles

An illustration of the insect's movement as it moves on expanding circles.

Step 1: Start

The insect begins at coordinates (1, 0) on the first circle, C₁ with a radius of 1.

Step 2: Move on C₁

The insect moves 1 radian counterclockwise along the circumference of circle C₁.

Step 3: Jump to C₂

The insect jumps to the next circle, C₂, which has a radius of 2. Its angle remains the same at the moment of the jump.

Step 4: Move on C₂

The insect now moves 2 radians counterclockwise along the circumference of circle C₂.

Step 5: Jump to C₃

The insect jumps to the next circle, C₃, with a radius of 3.

Step 6: Move on C₃

The insect moves 3 radians counterclockwise along circle C₃.

Step 7: Approaching the X-axis

The insect continues this pattern, moving on increasingly larger circles. Here, it's approaching the positive x-axis on a larger circle.

Step 8: Crossing the Positive X-axis

Finally, the insect crosses the positive x-axis. This occurs after moving 6 radians on the 6th circle and then jumping to the 7th circle, moving 1 more radian.

The insect first crosses the positive x-axis while moving on Circle C7.

This is an illustrated representation. The actual movement is continuous until the crossing occurs.
